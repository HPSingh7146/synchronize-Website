SYnCHronIZe

Synchronize is an Intra department IT fest organised by the final year students for their juniors. It encompasses a wide range of technical and non-technical events carefully crafted and conducted by the Computer Science Department.

IT Manager

Yatha Raja Tatha Praja (As the ruler does, so do the citizens)

Individual | 4 rounds

This event tests our potential IT Manafers of their kaushalya viveka and vichara by putting them into precarious situations. May the rightful leader don the crown!

Guidelines for IT Manager:

Round 1: Aptitude Test (Online)

  • Conducted in a controlled lab via Google Forms.
  • Strictly no calculators or mobile devices.
  • Complete within the set time frame.
  • Arrive 15 minutes early with ID, pen, and paper.

Round 2: Oratory Face-Off

  • Topic will be given a day before
  • Prepare a general debate structure focusing on introduction, arguments, and conclusion.
  • 8 minutes will be provided for each pair.

Round 3: The CEO Showdown

  • Craft and present an action plan for a hypothetical startup.
  • The case will be revealed just 10 minutes before your turn.
  • 5 will make it to the final battle.

Final Round: The HR Interview

  • A one-on-one interview to prove your mettle.
  • Resume is a must and be ready to discuss strengths, weaknesses, and past rounds..
  • Dress sharp, act sharper.

Agora

Inward seeking and simplistic Expression of Knowledge

Individual | 3 rounds

The stage is set for the participaants to seek and express their knowledge. (Listening, Remembering and Speaking)

Guidelines for Agora:

Round 1: Echo Chamber: Audio to Action

  • Audio will be played on spot based on that MCQ will be there
  • Time Limit: 30 mins
  • No. of Questions: 20

Round 2: Visual Connect: Tech and Ethics Edition

  • The Participants will engage with a dynamic PowerPoint presentation featuring a series of images.
  • Each slide will include a tech-related visual or an image associated with Indian ethics.
  • Participants must connect the images, discuss how they relate to each other, and build a coherent narrative throughout the presentation.
  • Judge's Decision Will Be Final.

Round 3: Newsroom : live clash

  • Participants will be grouped into pairs. Each pair will take on the roles of news reporters to debate with their pair.
  • The organizing team will provide each pair with a specific situation, atrocity, or tech-related issue aligned with the theme "Synchronizing Modern Tech with Indian Ethos."
  • One participant in each pair will argue the positive side of the issue, while the other will argue the negative side.

CodeVerse

Pratyaksa-anumana-agamah pramanani (Reverse coding, debugging, decoding)

Individual | 3 rounds

Get, set and code! The stage where your logical reasoning and problem solving skills are tested!

Guidelines for CodeVerse:

Round 1 (Preliminary):

  • Time Limit: 30 minutes
  • Number of Questions: 20
  • Topics Covered: Programming Concepts, Aptitude, Logical Thinking Ability and Basic Knowledge of Algorithms.
  • The top 30 scorers will be qualified to participate in the further rounds.
  • In case of a tie, time will be considered for qualification.

Round 2 (Reverse Coding / Decoding):

  • Time Limit: 40 minutes
  • Number of Questions: 4
  • Programming Languages: C, Java, Python
  • Topics Covered: The participants have to lookout and fix any missing piece of code or a block of code present in the already existing code and guess the purpose / output of the code.
  • The top 10 coders will qualify to the final round.
  • In case of a tie, time will be considered for qualification.

Round 3 (Coding):

  • Time Limit: 50 minutes
  • Number of Questions: 5
  • Programming Languages: C, Java, Python
  • The top 3 coders will be the final winners of the CodeVerse Event.
  • In case of a tie, time or quality of interpretation will be considered for qualification.

Synergia

Team Spirits and Collective Brilliance (relay)

Team | 3 rounds

The place where strongest bonds shine and collective knowledge come on top!

Guidelines for Synergia:

Round 1: Tech Image Identification

  • Objective: Identify blurred technology logos (e.g., Apple, Google).
  • Format: Teams have 30 seconds to identify each logo.
  • Scoring: 1 point per correct logo.
  • Advancement: Top 12 teams move to the next round.

Round 2: Collaborative Mini-Tasks:

  • Objective: Test teamwork and problem-solving.
  • Format: Each team member completes a mini-task (e.g., puzzles, coding) that contributes to the final group answer.
  • Scoring: Points based on the accuracy of the combined final answer.( Each team member earns 3 points for successfully completing their individual mini-task. The team will receive total 15 points for an accurate final combined answer.)
  • Advancement: Top 5 teams move to the next round.

Round 3: Risk-Based Quiz:

  • Objective: Balance risk and reward by selecting questions of varying difficulty (Easy, Medium, Hard).
  • Format: Teams choose questions based on confidence. Harder questions offer more points but carry penalties for incorrect answers.

Weblynx

Weaving Websites

Individual | 3 rounds

Showcase your web development skills

Guidelines for Synergia:

Round 1: WebSankalp(MCQ)

  • Participants will be given a set of MCQs based on basics of HTML, CSS and JS.
  • It will be held on Google Forms, so they have to use their college mail.
  • Switching of tabs will result in disqualification.
  • Time limit: 30mins

Round 2: WebShrishti

  • In this round we will give participants a design of a website which is in Desktop Size. They have to Develop the website in Mobile size.
  • Usage of any other website will lead to disqualification
  • The time constraint is 60 minutes.
  • Top 10 will proceed to the next round.

Final Round : WebKala

  • In this round , participants will be provided with a website design in PDF format, along with all necessary assets. The challenge is to meticulously replicate the provided design using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Beyond basic replication, participants are expected to enhance the website with interactive elements such as hover effects, click responses, and animations to bring the design to life.
  • The time constraint is 60 minutes.
  • This competition is a test of precision, creativity, and technical skill, pushing participants to not only match the design but also to add dynamic, user-friendly features that elevate the overall user experience.
  • The Final winner and 2 runner-ups are awarded.
