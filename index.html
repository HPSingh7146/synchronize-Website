IT Manager

Yatha Raja Tatha Praja (As the ruler does, so do the citizens)

Individual | 4 rounds

This event tests our potential IT Manafers of their kaushalya viveka and vichara by putting them into precarious situations. May the rightful leader don the crown!

Guidelines for IT Manager:

Round 1: Aptitude Test (Online)

  • Conducted in a controlled lab via Google Forms.
  • Strictly no calculators or mobile devices.
  • Complete within the set time frame.
  • Arrive 15 minutes early with ID, pen, and paper.

Round 2: Oratory Face-Off

  • Topic will be given a day before
  • Prepare a general debate structure focusing on introduction, arguments, and conclusion.
  • 8 minutes will be provided for each pair.

Round 3: The CEO Showdown

  • Craft and present an action plan for a hypothetical startup.
  • The case will be revealed just 10 minutes before your turn.
  • 5 will make it to the final battle.

Final Round: The HR Interview

  • A one-on-one interview to prove your mettle.
  • Resume is a must and be ready to discuss strengths, weaknesses, and past rounds..
  • Dress sharp, act sharper.

CodeVerse

Pratyaksa-anumana-agamah pramanani (Reverse coding, debugging, decoding)

Individual | 3 rounds

Get, set and code! The stage where your logical reasoning and problem solving skills are tested!

Guidelines for CodeVerse:

Round 1 (Preliminary):

  • Time Limit: 30 minutes
  • Number of Questions: 20
  • Topics Covered: Programming Concepts, Aptitude, Logical Thinking Ability and Basic Knowledge of Algorithms.
  • The top 30 scorers will be qualified to participate in the further rounds.
  • In case of a tie, time will be considered for qualification.

Round 2 (Reverse Coding / Decoding):

  • Time Limit: 40 minutes
  • Number of Questions: 4
  • Programming Languages: C, Java, Python
  • Topics Covered: The participants have to lookout and fix any missing piece of code or a block of code present in the already existing code and guess the purpose / output of the code.
  • The top 10 coders will qualify to the final round.
  • In case of a tie, time will be considered for qualification.

Round 3 (Coding):

  • Time Limit: 50 minutes
  • Number of Questions: 5
  • Programming Languages: C, Java, Python
  • The top 3 coders will be the final winners of the CodeVerse Event.
  • In case of a tie, time or quality of interpretation will be considered for qualification.

IoTize

Connecting sensors with solutions

Team | 3 rounds

Model and deploy IoT solutions!

Guidelines for IOTize:

Round 1: Quiz

  • Individual participation.
  • 30-minute quiz with 20 MCQs on IoT basics, protocols, sensors, security, and applications.
  • No use of electronic devices except for the quiz platform.
  • No negative marking
  • Top 10 participants advance to Round 2.

Round 2: Ideation

  • Form teams of 2 after Round 1 results.
  • Teaming up with seniors or those with IoT experience is recommended.
  • Receive a list of components and themes like "traffic system" or "water problem" prior to the event.
  • 30 minutes to prepare a PowerPoint presentation outlining the idea, components, and implementation plan during the event.
  • 5 minutes per team to present the idea.
  • Judges score based on uniqueness, clarity, feasibility, and presentation quality.
  • Top 5 teams advance to Round 3.

Round 3: Implementation

  • Build a prototype based on the idea presented in Round 2.
  • 1 hour to develop the prototype using provided components.
  • 5 minutes to present the prototype, explain the development process, and discuss implementation.
  • Judges score based on functionality, clarity of explanation, innovation, and practicality.
  • Final winners are determined based on overall performance.

Inspira

Knowledge Gathering and Creative Representation

Team | 2 rounds

Gather knowledge on topics, post it in socila media and the team with most comments wins!

Guidelines for Inspira:

Round 1: Rapid Rhapsody

  • Each team will randomly draw a topic that spans various tech domains.
  • Teams will have 5 minutes to research their selected topic using provided systems. They must gather as much relevant information as possible in this short time.
  • Usage of any type of AI tools are forbidden for the research.
  • After the research period, teams will participate in a 2-minute trivia challenge. They will be asked a series of questions related to their topic, with each requiring a one-word answer.
  • Each team is allowed 2 passes to skip questions they are unsure of, which they can use strategically to manage their time.

Round 2: TechTales

  • Each team will randomly draw a technological concept and a creative scenario through which they must explain
  • Teams will have 5 minutes to brainstorm and prepare their explanation. They can use props, drawings, or any other creative methods provided to them.
  • Each team will then have 3 minutes to present their explanation to the judges, using the scenario to make the concept understandable and engaging.

Round 3: Insta Twist

  • Each team will randomly draw a twist that they must incorporate into their Instagram post. These twists could involve specific constraints or creative prompts.
  • Along with the twist, teams will receive a modern technology concept to focus on.
  • Teams will design an Instagram post, using the twist and the assigned theme to create something unique and compelling.
  • Teams must write a caption that not only explains the cultural and tech elements but also ties in the twist they were given.
  • Teams will present their Instagram post and caption to the judges, explaining how they adapted to the twist and what message they aimed to convey.
